IMPORTANT INFORMATION:

The clinical comments are based on Australian Product Information, available at the Australian Register of Therapeutic Goods website. The suggested actions are based on the NIH Drug-Drug Interactions Between Ritonavir-Boosted Nirmatrelvir (Paxlovid) and the Concomitant Medications {link https://www.covid19treatmentguidelines.nih.gov/therapies/antiviral-therapy/ritonavir-boosted-nirmatrelvir--paxlovid-/paxlovid-drug-drug-interactions/ } and Liverpool COVID-19 Interactions Checker {link https://www.covid19-druginteractions.org/checker}. For some medicines, data on nirmatrelvir plus ritonavir interaction is limited.

Contraindicated - Consider alternative COVID-19 treatment. For these medicines, there is a risk of serious side effects or reduced treatment efficacy, and withholding medicines is not an effective or feasible strategy

Cease temporarily - Withhold medicine. For these medicines, there is a risk of serious adverse events. Only start nirmatrelvir plus ritonavir if the medicine can be safely stopped. The medicine can be resumed 3 days after treatment completion. They may need to be withheld for longer if the patient is elderly or the medication has a long half-life.

Reduce dose - If medicine cannot be safely stopped or replaced, adjust the dose and monitor for potential side effects. Information about individual dosing can be found at the Liverpool COVID-19 Drug Interactions website

Monitor - Dose adjustment is not required, but may be considered. Educate your patient about potential side effects and advise to stop this medicine if needed.
